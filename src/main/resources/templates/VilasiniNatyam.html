Vilasini Natyam is an ancient Telugu dance tradition of the hereditary Devadasis (Kalavantulu), performed in temples, royal courts, and public spaces, focusing on detailed abhinaya (expression) and incorporating ritual, courtly, and folk elements, revived and presented in its comprehensive form by Guru Swapnasundari in the late 20th century. It features distinct adavus (steps) and a rich vocabulary of seated abhinaya, distinct from other classical forms like Bharatanatyam, offering a unique blend of ritualistic devotion and aesthetic artistry.
